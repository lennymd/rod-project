From engineering, to journalism, to design & Delft

I came to TU Delft after completing a Master’s in Photography from Syracuse University and teaching for two years at the University of Miami. Before that, I earned my bachelor’s at MIT. In my engineering coursework, I learned how to tackle complex situations with seemingly unrelated components. I developed an interest in identifying underlying trends in these situations that would tie the components into common equations. The equations illuminated the importance of the different variables and allowed me to gain a more in-depth understanding of their relations. Outside my courses, photography was my hobby; I enjoyed how a camera could make tangible the emotions, shapes, and relationships in a scene.

I built on my interests in identifying trends and relationships during my master’s coursework in photography and interactive media. The flexibility of the program granted me the opportunity to work across different media to explore the nuances of relationships: I learned to produce and edit stories with photographs, video, animation, augmented and virtual reality, and data visualization.

For my graduation project, I presented the results from three endeavors I undertook over the course of a year and a half: a journalism fellowship investigating hate crimes in America, a semester as a full-time teaching assistant for a product design course at MIT, and my work to independently produce and develop a data visualization story about Formula One teams.

After graduation, I transitioned to teaching courses at the University of Miami. In working with both bachelor’s and master’s students, I further developed my abilities to use technology to characterize and understand underlying trends and communicate the nuances to the students by developing course materials (syllabi, lectures, assignments, tutorials, videos) that emphasized building a common knowledge base and allowing room for students to further explore their own interests.

I came to TU Delft to expand my abilities to break down and understand a situation and to work towards a PhD, so I can do my own teaching and research work.

I made this site to collect the different facets of my practice in one place. Not every post is long, but each post speaks to the kind of practicioner I am and the things I value. It's made by hand and there may be dead links. If you find one, let me know. All my contact info is on my website.
